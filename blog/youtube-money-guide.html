CPM vs RPM — What's the Difference?

Before we dive into numbers, you need to understand two key terms:

CPM (Cost Per Mille)

CPM is what advertisers pay per 1,000 ad impressions. This is the "advertiser side" number. CPM can range from $0.50 to $50+ depending on niche and audience location.

RPM (Revenue Per Mille)

RPM is what you actually earn per 1,000 video views. RPM is always lower than CPM because YouTube takes a 45% cut, and not every view has an ad shown on it. RPM = roughly 45–55% of CPM.

Simple Rule: If your CPM is $4, your RPM is roughly $1.80–$2.20. For every 1,000 views, you earn $1.80–$2.20.

Other Ways YouTubers Make Money

AdSense is just one revenue stream. Successful YouTubers earn from multiple sources:

  • Sponsorships — Brand deals often pay 10–20x more than AdSense per video
  • Affiliate marketing — Commission from products mentioned in videos
  • Merchandise — Selling branded products to fans
  • Channel memberships — Monthly subscription from loyal viewers
  • Super Chats — Paid messages during live streams
  • Courses & digital products — Selling knowledge to audience

How to Increase Your YouTube RPM

  • Target US/UK/CA audience — Use English content, reference American topics
  • Choose high-CPM niches — Finance, tech, B2B content pays more
  • Enable all ad types — Skippable, non-skippable, bumper, overlay ads
  • Longer videos — 8+ minute videos can have mid-roll ads (more ad slots)
  • Post in Q4 — Oct–Dec has highest CPM rates (holiday advertising)

YouTube Partner Program Requirements

To monetize with AdSense on YouTube, you need:

  • 1,000 subscribers
  • 4,000 watch hours in the past 12 months (or 10M Shorts views)
  • No active Community Guidelines strikes
  • Live in an eligible country
