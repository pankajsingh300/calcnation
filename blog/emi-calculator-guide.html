What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Instalment. It is the fixed amount you pay every month to repay a loan. Each EMI has two components:

  • Principal component — repays the original loan amount
  • Interest component — pays the interest charged by the bank

In the early months, most of your EMI goes towards interest. As the loan progresses, more of each EMI goes towards the principal. This is called amortization.

The EMI Formula

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)ⁿ / [(1 + r)ⁿ − 1]

Where:

  • P = Principal loan amount (₹)
  • r = Monthly interest rate = Annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100
  • n = Loan tenure in months

Step-by-Step EMI Calculation Example

Example: ₹50 Lakh Home Loan at 8.5% for 20 Years

  • P = ₹50,00,000
  • Annual rate = 8.5%, so monthly rate r = 8.5 ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = 0.007083
  • Tenure = 20 years = 240 months

Step 1: Calculate (1 + r)ⁿ = (1.007083)²⁴⁰ = 5.3133

Step 2: EMI = 50,00,000 × 0.007083 × 5.3133 ÷ (5.3133 − 1)

Step 3: EMI = 50,00,000 × 0.037645 ÷ 4.3133

Result: EMI ≈ ₹43,391/month

Total Interest You Pay — The Shocking Truth

Most people focus only on the EMI amount. But the total interest paid over the full loan tenure is often more than the loan itself!

₹50 Lakh Loan at 8.5% for 20 Years

  • Monthly EMI: ₹43,391
  • Total payments: ₹43,391 × 240 = ₹1,04,13,840
  • Total interest paid: ₹1,04,13,840 − ₹50,00,000 = ₹54,13,840

You pay more in interest than the original loan amount! This is why prepayment and shorter tenures save enormous amounts.

How to Reduce Your Home Loan EMI

1. Make a Higher Down Payment

A 30–40% down payment instead of 20% reduces your principal significantly, lowering both EMI and total interest.

2. Choose a Longer Tenure

A 25-year tenure has lower EMI than 20 years — but you pay far more interest overall. Only choose longer tenure if cash flow is tight.

3. Prepay Whenever Possible

Even one extra EMI per year can reduce your total tenure by 2–3 years and save lakhs in interest. Most banks allow part-prepayment without penalty on floating-rate loans.

4. Negotiate a Lower Interest Rate

A 0.5% reduction in interest rate on ₹50L over 20 years saves approximately ₹3.5–4 lakh in total interest. Always compare rates from multiple banks before taking a loan.

5. Transfer Your Loan (Balance Transfer)

If rates have dropped since you took the loan, a balance transfer to a bank offering lower rates can reduce your EMI significantly.

EMI vs Interest-Only Payments

Some loans offer interest-only periods (moratorium). During this time you pay only interest, not principal. This keeps payments low initially but doesn't reduce the principal — so your loan tenure effectively extends.
