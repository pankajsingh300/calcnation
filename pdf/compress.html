What is PDF Compression?

PDF compression reduces the file size of your PDF document by optimizing the internal structure, compressing embedded images, and removing unnecessary metadata. This makes files easier to share via email, upload to websites, or store on devices with limited space.

Our tool processes everything in your browser using JavaScript — your file never leaves your device, making it completely private and secure. Unlike other tools, we don't upload your documents to any server.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my PDF uploaded to a server? ▾
No. Everything happens entirely in your browser. Your PDF file never leaves your device and is never uploaded to any server. This makes our tool completely private and secure.
How much can I compress a PDF? ▾
Results vary by file type. PDFs with many images can often be reduced by 40–70%. Text-heavy PDFs may only reduce by 10–20% since text is already efficiently stored.
Will compression reduce PDF quality? ▾
Lower compression levels preserve quality well. Higher compression may slightly reduce image sharpness. For documents, quality loss is usually invisible at normal zoom levels.
Is there a file size limit? ▾
We support PDFs up to 100MB. Larger files may take longer to process depending on your device's speed. All processing is done locally so speed depends on your computer.
Is this tool really free? ▾
Yes, completely free forever. No subscription, no watermarks, no limits on how many times you can use it. We earn through advertising shown on the page.
